Video Capture Guidelines for Clinic Nurses
How to record the clinic walkthrough video for audit — mandatory protocol
MANDATORY ▼
Purpose: The clinic walkthrough video is the only source of information for AI audit evaluation. If something is not captured properly in the video, the AI cannot confirm its presence. Nurses must follow this protocol strictly to avoid incorrect deductions due to poor recording.
📋 Step-by-Step Recording Protocol
  • 1
    Start from Outside / Entrance: Begin recording from outside the clinic building. Walk slowly toward the entrance. Capture the full entrance area — ground, door, outside walls. Ensure no garbage or waste at entrance is missed. Hold steady for at least 4–5 seconds at the entrance.
  • 2
    Capture the Outside M-Swasth Board: Point the camera directly at the outside board. Zoom in slightly so the tagline, doctor image, and branding are clearly readable. Hold for a minimum of 3 seconds. Do not walk past it quickly. If the board is high up, angle the camera upward and hold steady.
  • 3
    Enter the Clinic & Capture Inside Banner: After entering, immediately pan to show the inside M-Swasth banner. Zoom in on the tagline, doctor image, mobile screen, timing slot, and e-clinic number. Hold for at least 3 seconds. Ensure the full banner is in frame — top to bottom.
  • 4
    Capture All 8 Mandatory Posters — One by One: Walk to each poster individually. Hold the camera steady in front of each poster for a minimum of 3 seconds. Zoom in enough so the poster title/topic is clearly readable. Do not pan all posters in one sweeping shot — each poster must be shown separately. Confirm all 8 are captured before moving on.
  • 5
    Capture All 4 Certificates: Point camera at the certificates display area. Zoom in on each certificate — Doctor, Pharmacist, Nurse, and M-Swasth. Hold each for 3 seconds. Ensure the certificate name/type is clearly visible in frame.
  • 6
    Capture Walls, Floor & Ceiling: Slowly pan across all four walls. Show the floor from multiple angles. Briefly look upward to capture ceiling/upper wall area. Check for stains, cobwebs, peeling paint, or damage. Walk slowly — do not rush this section.
  • 7
    Capture Lighting Conditions: While walking through the clinic, ensure the ambient lighting is visible. Do not use flash — let the natural/artificial lighting be captured as-is. The AI will assess lighting conditions from this footage.
  • 8
    Capture Table & Furniture Setup: Show the full table from the front and side. Zoom in on the green cloth — check that it fully covers the table. Show both chairs/stools clearly. Hold for 3–4 seconds on the table and again on the chairs.
  • 9
    Capture Medicine Storage & Equipment: Open the trunk or show the medicine rack clearly. Zoom in to show how medicines are arranged. Show the BP machine, weighing machine, and any other equipment. Zoom in on each piece of equipment. Hold each for 3 seconds. If medicines are on the table, capture them clearly too.
  • 10
    Final Wide Shot: End the video with a slow 360° pan of the entire clinic interior so the AI can assess the overall setup, space, and general organization from a single wide-angle view.
🔑 8 Golden Rules for Video Recording
  • ⏱️
    Hold every item for at least 3 seconds: Boards, banners, each poster, each certificate, table, medicines, equipment — all must be held steady for a minimum of 3 seconds. Fast panning will cause AI to mark items as "not captured."
  • 🔍
    Zoom in on text and branding: Taglines, poster titles, certificate names, medicine labels — zoom in slightly so text is readable. The AI reads content from the video; blurry text cannot be verified.
  • 🚶
    Walk slowly and steadily: Move at a slow walking pace throughout the clinic. Do not rush or jog. Shaky or fast-moving footage cannot be analyzed accurately.
  • 💡
    Do not use flash or torch light: Use existing clinic lighting only. Flash creates glare and hides actual lighting conditions from the AI assessment.
  • 📐
    Keep camera level and straight: Hold the phone horizontally where possible. Keep the camera at eye level. Avoid extreme angles that cut off top or bottom of boards, banners, or posters.
  • 📝
    All 8 posters must be individually captured: Do not group multiple posters in a single shot. Each poster must be a separate, clear, held shot. Missing even one poster shot will result in a deduction.
  • 🔄
    Follow the sequence: Outside → Board → Enter → Banner → Posters (1–8) → Certificates → Walls/Floor → Table → Medicines/Equipment → Final wide shot. Skipping steps will result in those items being marked as "not captured."
  • 📵
    Do not cut or pause recording mid-sequence: Record the entire walkthrough as one continuous video. Cutting and restarting the video creates confusion in sequence verification for the AI.
🤖 How AI Evaluates the Video — AI Check Criteria
  • CHECK 1
    Hold Duration Check: AI analyzes frame sequences to confirm each item was held steady for at least 3 seconds. Items with less than 3 seconds of stable focus will be flagged as "Insufficiently captured — cannot confirm."
  • CHECK 2
    Zoom Adequacy Check: AI checks if text elements (taglines, poster titles, certificate names) are legible in the frame. If text is too small or blurry to read, AI will note "Not clearly visible due to poor zoom/video quality — no deduction applied" but will flag for re-capture.
  • CHECK 3
    Blur & Quality Check: If a frame is blurry due to camera movement, AI will NOT deduct points for that item — it will instead note "Not clearly visible due to poor video quality/angle — no deduction applied." Deductions only apply when content is clearly visible and clearly wrong.
  • CHECK 4
    Coverage Check: AI verifies that all required items have been covered in the video — all 8 posters, 4 certificates, outside board, inside banner, table, medicines, entrance. Any item not shown at all will be flagged as "Not captured in video" and may attract a deduction.
  • CHECK 5
    Sequence Logic Check: AI checks if the video follows a logical walkthrough sequence. Random or jumbled sequences may result in some items being missed or double-counted.
  • CHECK 6
    Content Verification Check: For visible content (taglines, logos, cloth color, storage type), AI reads and verifies against the standard reference. Only issues that are clearly and confidently visible will result in deductions.
  • CHECK 7
    Missing Area Flags: If entire areas are skipped (e.g., entrance never shown, no poster wall visible), AI will note this as a gap and apply appropriate deductions for items "not captured in video."
❌ Common Mistakes That Cause Score Loss
  • ✗
    Walking past the outside board without stopping — AI cannot read the tagline or verify branding
  • ✗
    Capturing all 8 posters in one wide sweeping pan — individual posters cannot be identified and verified separately
  • ✗
    Starting video from inside the clinic — entrance and outside board are missed entirely
  • ✗
    Recording in dim lighting or using flash — lighting condition cannot be assessed accurately
  • ✗
    Not zooming in on the green cloth — cloth condition (dirty/wrinkled/partial coverage) cannot be confirmed
  • ✗
    Stopping video before showing medicines and equipment — storage type and organization cannot be evaluated
  • ✗
    Recording from too far away — certificate names and poster titles become unreadable
  • ✗
    Holding phone vertically (portrait mode) — cuts off wide items like boards, banners, and full wall views

1. Cleanliness (Inside & Entrance)
Interior walls · floors · shelves · ceiling · entrance · lighting
20 / 20 ▼
Definition: This parameter evaluates the overall hygiene and presentation of the clinic — including its internal environment (walls, floor, shelves, ceiling) and the immediate outside entrance area. Adequate lighting is also assessed here. A clean, well-lit clinic reflects professional standards and patient safety.
📐 Sub-Parameters & What to Check
Sub-Parameter What Must Be Observed Max Points
Entrance Area No garbage, waste, or debris at the entrance or immediately outside the clinic door. Ground should be visibly clean. 4 pts
Walls (Inside) Walls must be free from stains, marks, dirt, or peeling paint. All four walls should be checked. Paint must look maintained and clean. 4 pts
Floor (Inside) Floor must be swept and clean. No visible waste, spills, or damage. No broken tiles or hazardous surfaces. 4 pts
Shelves & Surfaces All shelves, racks, and horizontal surfaces must be free from dust, cobwebs, and accumulated dirt. Items on shelves should look organized. 3 pts
Ceiling / Upper Walls No cobwebs or dirt visible on ceiling or upper wall areas. Corners should be checked. Clean ceiling is expected. included in walls
Lighting Clinic interior must be adequately lit — either through natural light (windows/doors) or functional artificial lights. No dim, dark, or unlit sections inside the clinic. 5 pts
⚠️ Deduction Table — Cleanliness
Issue Found Points Deducted
Garbage or waste clearly visible at entrance −4 pts
Dirty/stained walls or peeling paint clearly visible inside −4 pts
Dirty/damaged floor or visible waste/debris inside clinic −4 pts
Dust, cobwebs, or dirty shelves/surfaces clearly visible −3 pts
Poor or dim lighting clearly visible inside the clinic −5 pts
🤖 AI Rule: Only deduct when the issue is clearly and confidently visible in the video. If a wall is blurry or partially shown, do not assume it is dirty. If lighting appears dim but video quality is poor, note "Not clearly visible due to poor video quality — no deduction applied." Multiple issues result in cumulative deductions. Minimum score = 0.
🎥 Video Capture Note: Nurse must slowly pan all four walls, show the floor from at least 2 angles, and capture the entrance area clearly. Ceiling/upper walls should be briefly shown. Lighting must be captured without flash.

2. Board & Banner (M-Swasth Only)
Outside board + inside banner — branding, tagline, condition & placement
20 / 20 ▼
Definition: This parameter evaluates whether the clinic has the correct and up-to-date M-Swasth branding — both outside (board) and inside (banner). Scoring covers presence, correctness of tagline, visual condition, proper placement, and completeness of required elements. Partner clinic branding (Namra, Muthoot, etc.) is ignored — only M-Swasth branding is scored.
🔍 Search Location Logic:
1. First check for M-Swasth board outside the clinic building.
2. If a partner board (e.g., Namra, Muthoot) is present outside → this is an in-house clinic → M-Swasth board/banner expected inside.
3. Check both locations before concluding board is missing.
4. Ignore all partner boards for scoring — only evaluate M-Swasth branding.

4. Furniture & Table Setup
Minimum furniture · green cloth coverage · table condition
20 / 20 ▼
Definition: This parameter verifies that the clinic has the minimum required furniture (1 table + 2 chairs/stools) and that the main clinic table is properly covered with a green cloth. The green cloth must be clean, smooth, and fully covering the table surface. Any shade of green is acceptable.
📐 Sub-Parameters & What to Check
Sub-Parameter What Must Be Observed Impact if Failed
Table Presence At least 1 table must be present in the clinic consultation area. It must be a proper table — not a makeshift surface. Score = 0 if absent
Chairs / Stools Minimum 2 chairs or stools must be present — one for the nurse/operator and one for the patient. Both must be clearly visible in the video. Score = 0 if fewer than 2
Green Cloth — Presence The main clinic table must have a green cloth covering. Any shade of green is acceptable (dark green, light green, olive, etc.). −10 pts if absent
Green Cloth — Coverage The cloth must fully cover the table surface — edges and all corners. Partial coverage (table legs or sides visible) is not acceptable. −5 pts if partial
Green Cloth — Condition Cloth must be clean — no visible stains, heavy dirt marks. Must be spread smoothly — not heavily wrinkled or bunched up. −5 pts if dirty/wrinkled
Overall Table Setup Table should look organized and well-maintained — not cluttered with non-clinic items or in a state of disarray. −3 pts if poorly maintained
⚠️ Deduction Table — Furniture & Table Setup
Issue Found Points Deducted
No table found in clinic Score = 0
Fewer than 2 chairs or stools present Score = 0
No green cloth on the table −10 pts
Green cloth present but clearly dirty, heavily stained, or visibly very wrinkled −5 pts
Green cloth does not fully cover table — table surface partially exposed −5 pts
Table setup present but poorly maintained or disorganized −3 pts
🤖 AI Rule: Score = 0 is applied only when table or chairs are completely absent. For green cloth issues, only deduct when clearly and confidently visible in the video. If cloth is partially out of frame, do not assume it doesn't cover the table.
🎥 Video Capture Note: Nurse must show the full table from the front — then zoom in on the green cloth to show it fully covers all edges. Chairs must both be visible in the frame. Hold each item for 3–4 seconds. Zoom in on cloth condition to allow AI to assess cleanliness.

5. Placement of Medicine & Equipment
OTC medicine storage · trunk/rack organization · equipment condition
20 / 20 ▼
Definition: This parameter evaluates how medicines and medical equipment are stored and organized. Only essential OTC medicines should be visible on the table; all stock must be properly stored in a trunk or medicine rack — not in cardboard boxes. Equipment must be clean, functional-looking, and neatly placed.
📐 Sub-Parameters & What to Check
Sub-Parameter What Must Be Observed Impact if Failed
Medicine on Table Only essential OTC medicines should be visible on the consultation table. A small, neat quantity is acceptable. Cluttered or excessive stock on table is not allowed. −5 pts if cluttered
Medicine Storage Type All bulk medicine stock must be stored inside a proper trunk or medicine rack. Cardboard boxes or cartons are strictly not allowed as medicine storage. −5 pts for carton/cardboard
Medicine Organization Medicines inside trunk/rack must be neatly arranged — not thrown loosely or piled randomly. Labels should be facing outward where possible. −3 pts if untidy
Expired Medicines No medicines with expired dates should be visible. If any medicine with a visibly expired date is found during video, it must be flagged. −6 pts if found
Equipment — BP Machine BP machine must be present, visibly clean, and properly placed — not thrown loosely or buried under items. −4 pts if dirty/misplaced
Equipment — Weighing Machine & Others Weighing machine and any other equipment must be visible, clean, and neatly arranged. Equipment must not look dusty, broken, or mishandled. −4 pts if dirty/misplaced
🏥 In-House Clinic Exception: In-house clinics (partner-run) may keep a small quantity of medicines on or near the table for distribution purposes. Do NOT deduct for this only if the quantity is small and arrangement looks neat and organized. Deduct only if it appears messy, cluttered, or poorly organized.
⚠️ Deduction Table — Medicine & Equipment
Issue Found Points Deducted
Medicine stock kept in cardboard/carton box (not a trunk or rack) −5 pts
Cluttered or disorganized medicines visible on table −5 pts
Equipment dirty, dusty, or poorly placed −4 pts
Medicines clearly expired or improperly stored (visible expiry) −6 pts
General untidy or disorganized arrangement clearly visible −3 pts
🤖 AI Rule: For in-house clinics, apply exception only if arrangement looks neat. For expired medicines, only flag if an expiry date is clearly visible and readable in the video. Do not assume medicines are expired without clearly seeing a date. Deductions are cumulative.
🎥 Video Capture Note: Nurse must open the trunk or show the medicine rack clearly. Zoom in on medicine arrangement. Show the BP machine and weighing machine individually with a 3-second hold each. If medicines are on the table, zoom in on the arrangement so AI can assess tidiness.
📜 General Evaluation Rules
📜
General Rules for AI Agents & Re-Audit Staff
Core evaluation principles — must be followed strictly for all audits
RULES ▼
🔒 Core Rules
  • 1
    No assumptions allowed: If the video is blurry, angle is poor, or something is not clearly visible, do NOT assume it is missing or incorrect. Only deduct points when you are sure based on what is clearly visible.
  • 2
    Be strict on all parameters: Do not give benefit of doubt unless video quality is genuinely the limiting factor. If something is clearly visible and clearly wrong, apply the deduction.
  • 3
    Every deduction needs a reason: Every deduction must be accompanied by a specific comment explaining exactly what was wrong AND whether it was not captured, not visible due to video quality, or missing entirely.
  • 4
    Name specific items in deductions: For posters and certificates, always name the specific item (e.g., "P3 — Medicine Storage Rules poster not visible in video"). Generic remarks like "posters missing" are not acceptable.
  • 5
    Minimum score is 0: No individual category score can go below 0, even with cumulative deductions exceeding 20 points.
  • 6
    Cumulative deductions: Multiple issues within the same category result in cumulative deductions (added together). Do not cap at one deduction per category.
  • 7
    Partner branding is ignored: Only M-Swasth branding is scored in Section 2. Namra, Muthoot, or any other partner board/banner is not evaluated for scoring purposes.
  • 8
    Regional language posters accepted: Posters in Hindi, regional languages, or English are all acceptable if the topic is identifiable. Do not deduct for language differences alone.
  • 9
    In-house clinic exception applies: Apply the in-house medicine exception only if arrangement is neat. Sloppy or cluttered in-house medicine arrangements still attract deductions.
  • 10
    Final score is out of 100: All 5 category scores (each out of 20) are added to give the final score. Report as XX/100 with star rating.

📝 Final Notes & Evaluator Reminders
  • 🔍Use only factual deductions — do not speculate or make assumptions based on poor video quality
  • ✍️Key Observations must include only what was actually seen — both Good Practices and Issues to Improve
  • 📋Every comment/remark must clearly mention: reason for deduction AND whether item was not captured, not visible, or clearly missing
  • 🏷️Always name specific posters and certificates when applying deductions — generic remarks are not acceptable
  • 💯Final score must be reported out of 100 with star rating from ★☆☆☆☆ to ★★★★★
  • 📹If video quality is consistently poor throughout, flag this clearly in the summary for re-capture request
  • 📩All evaluations must be strictly based on the above parameters — deductions applied only per clearly visible conditions
